\section{Introduction}

This paper notably examines AI-assisted academic writing workflows.
These workflows can create repeated claims across independently drafted sections; this repetition is often hard to notice during revision.
Sentence-level review gives the human author a practical gate for accepting, revising, or rejecting each sentence.

\section{Method}

The prototype extracts sentences from a LaTeX draft and presents one sentence at a time.
It highlights adverbs, uncommon word candidates, and special punctuation such as hyphens, colons, and semicolons.
When requested, an AI model provides a constrained diagnosis rather than an automatic rewrite.

\section{Discussion}

This design intentionally slows down the review process.
The goal is not to maximize automation, but to help the author recover ownership of every sentence.
